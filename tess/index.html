User’s Guide to Tess¶

Tess is a Python package that supports three basic, and related, analysis tasks:

  1. Adaptively binning a spatial dataset to maximize resolution and S/N.
  2. Estimating the density field in a spatial dataset
  3. Rendering a density field, or any other field, using the tessellation.

Inspiration¶

Tess grew directly from the inspiration of Cappellari & Copin (2003), which introduced the Centroidal Voronoi Tessellation to astronomy. The methods used for estimating 2D density fields with Delaunay tessellations (i.e., the DTFE) are based on Schaap & van de Weygaert (2000).
